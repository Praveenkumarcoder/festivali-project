Why Good Friday is celebrated?


Many Christians around the world observe Good Friday on the Friday before Easter Sunday. It commemorates Jesus Christ's Passion, crucifixion, and death, which is told in the Christian Bible. It is the day after Maundy Thursday. Good Friday commemorates Jesus' death on the cross.

For many Christians, Good Friday is a time to remember the suffering and dying of Jesus before marking the resurrection of Christ on Easter Sunday. ... In his message for Lent 2020, Pope Francis told his followers that by contemplating Christ's sacrifice, they can reflect on their own sins and find mercy.
